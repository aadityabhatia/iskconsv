ISKCON of Silicon Valley is pleased to announce the initiation of its fund raising drive for the establishment of a new temple for Lord Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. The present ISKCON temple, the Sri Sri Radha Madan-Mohan Temple in West San Jose, needs to relocate to a new site in order to accommodate the rapidly growing congregation of Krishna devotees in the South Bay.

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) was founded by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. Its mission is to promote the well being of society by teaching the science of Krishna Consciousness according to the Bhagavad Gita and other ancient Vedic scriptures of India. It is a cultural movement for the spiritualization of human society.

The mission of ISKCON of Silicon Valley is To show mercy to all living beings by distributing the holy names of Krishna.

For the past six years, ISKCON devotees of Silicon Valley have been congregating for worship in the Radha Madan-Mohan Temple in a storefront in West San Jose. Although this site has served the congregation well, it is a rental facility with limited space to accommodate the needs of a rapidly expanding congregation of devotees. Furthermore, since the site is on a lease, the devotees desire that Lord Krishna have a place of His own where they can be permanently housed.

Accordingly, the purpose of this fund-raising drive is to raise sufficient funds to establish a new and permanent temple for Lord Krishna in the Silicon Valley. Our goal is to raise 1.5 Million Dollars to successfully accomplish this project. The new temple would serve as a center of ISKCON of Silicon Valley and provide a permanent place of worship for devotees of Lord Krishna. Additionally, it would offer to you and your family: devotional service, kirtan, Deity worship, prasadam and education in Krishna consciousness.
